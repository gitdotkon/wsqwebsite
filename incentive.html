Qualifying Qingdao Production Expenditures (QQPE)

The full criteria for the Qualifying Qingdao Production Expenditures (QQPE) will be announced and published by November 2016. A general summary of key technical criteria for international and domestic productions are found below. All terms are subject to change without notice.

Incentives for Film and TV Productions will involve a generous percentage cash back on qualifying production expenditures incurred in RMB in the Qingdao Oriental Movie Metropolis.

Eligible Productions:

Either a film or television project. The minimum running time of a film project is 60 minutes (or no less than 45 minutes for a large-format like IMAX). The minimum running time of a television project is 30 minutes per episode, inclusive of commercial and public service insertions.

Excluded productions:

Films that are fully or primarily animated, reality programs or game shows, commercials and advertisements, music videos, news and current affairs programs, magazine shows, talk shows, quiz shows, panel shows, variety shows or similar entertainment programs, any program consisting of a competition or contest or the results of a competition or contest, sports and public events coverage, productions with a primary purpose of fun-raising, training or in-house corporate advertising or promotions.

Cultural Test:

To qualify for the International Rebate there is no “cultural test” which a production must pass and no minimum period of time that needs to be spent on shooting or other film-making activities in the Qingdao Region.

Minimum Expenditure:

International films: minimal QQPE equals or exceeds US$15,000,000, and at least 50% of QQPE must be QQPE(OMM) expenditure in the Qingdao Oriental Movie Metropolis.

Domestic films: minimal QQPE equals or exceeds RMB¥15,000,000, and at least 50% of QQPE must be QQPE(OMM) expenditure in the Qingdao Oriental Movie Metropolis.

International television: minimal QQPE equals or exceeds US$1,000,000 per broadcast slot of one hour, and at least 50% of QQPE must be QQPE(OMM)--expenditure in Qingdao Oriental Movie Metropolis.

Domestic television: minimal QQPE equals or exceeds RMB¥1,000,000 per broadcast slot of one hour, and at least 50% of QQPE must be QQPE(OMM)--expenditure in Qingdao Oriental Movie Metropolis.

International ATL Cap: The QQPE for Above The Line (ATL) expenditures should be no more than USD$30 million or no more than 30% of total QQPE, whichever is less.

Qualified Expenditures:

‘QQPE’ are non-refundable expenditures incurred by the foreign or domestic producer on production activities in relation to the applicable eligible production, and attributable to goods and services used and consumed in the Qingdao region (including the use of land or properties located in the Qingdao region) and which is not otherwise excluded from QQPE as provided below. The nationality of those providing goods and services has no bearing on whether the expenditure qualifies as QQPE or QQPE (OMM). The “used and consumed” test does not focus on the supplier of goods or services but instead concentrates on the recipient or customer. In order to establish whether an item of expenditure qualifies as QQPE or QQPE (OMM) it is necessary to establish the nature of the specific goods and services in question and the place where the recipient uses or consumes those goods and services.

Qualifying expenditures include:

Accommodation costs (Qingdao region), audit fees (for rebate application purpose only), bank fees (Qingdao region), cast costs (for services in Qingdao; housing expenses included), catering, childcare, closed caption expenditure (prior to completion), company set up costs and expenses, completion guarantee costs and takeover costs (the lesser of (a) 1.5% of QQPE; or (b) 50% of the total fee payable to the completion guarantor), computer-generated imagery expenditure (Qingdao region), contingency, copyright acquisition, crew (for services in Qingdao; housing expenses included), deliverables, equipment, freight, insurance, location hire and other localized services expenditures, music, overhead, props expenditures, SAFE, SARFT, translation cost, travel (including car hire), versioning expenditures, Visas, voice coaching, withholding taxes.

Excluded from qualification:

Capital goods, contingent payments, credit card interests, development expenditures, distribution expenditures, entertainment expenditures, finance expenditures, goods and services tax/VAT，gratuities, legal fees and expenses, marketing, publicity and promotion expenditures, payroll taxes, test screening.

Eligible Applicants:

  • The Domestic Producer must be responsible for the film and needs to be a company formed under the laws of People’s Republic of China. The Domestic Producer must have the appropriate Film/Television production permit issued by the State Administration of Press, Publication, Radio, Film and Television (SARFT) and/or its relevant subdivisions having jurisdiction.
  • The Domestic Producer must have entered into an agreement (the ‘Production Agreement’) with one or more legal entities formed outside of the People’s Republic of China (the ‘Foreign Producer’):
    • the Domestic Producer and the Foreign Producer jointly invest (including funds, labour and/or in-kind contributions) in the applicable production (usually with neither party investing more than 80% or less than 20% of the cost of production) and jointly produce the applicable production, with both parties sharing profits and losses in accordance with their agreement;
    • only the Foreign Producer will invest in and produce the applicable production, while the Domestic Producer will provide and receive remuneration for necessary assistance to the Foreign Producer (e.g. providing equipment, facilities and labour) but no share of the copyright in the applicable production;
    • the Foreign Producer will fully entrust the Domestic Producer to produce the applicable production and receive remuneration for its services but the Domestic Producer shall have no share of the copyright in the applicable production.

Application Procedure

To apply for the International Rebate, the Applicant must, at the Applicant’s expense, complete the Application and send it to the Committee together with the following:

  • a copy of the Film Permit;
  • a copy of the fully executed Production Agreement;
  • a copy of either a Sino-foreign joint film production permit or approval of SARFT
  • a budget
  • the Applicant’s estimate of the likely QQPE
  • the Financial Support Documents;
  • a copy of the business licence of the Domestic Producer;
  • Both the Application and all of the Submission Materials must be delivered to the Committee by no later than 60 days prior to the Applicant’s anticipated start date of principal photography in the Qingdao region.
    Within 30 days after the Committee has received the fully completed Application and all of the Submission Materials and, if applicable, agreed the matters referred to in Section 6.4, the Committee will either
    • notify the Domestic Producer that the International Rebate is not available to the Applicant specifying the reasons for its determination ; or
    • send (on behalf of the Fund) to the Domestic Producer a completed International Rebate Certificate confirming that the International Rebate is available to the Domestic Producer and specifying

The following relating to the applicable production:

  • the Anticipated QQPE in RMB;
  • the Applicable Exchange Rate;
  • the Threshold Rate; and
  • the Maximum International Rebate Amount in RMB; and
  • the law that will govern the International Rebate Contract and the arbitration body that will determine disputes under the International Rebate Contract

Payment

The Applicant shall make such a claim by submitting to the Committee the following:

  • a completed application form
  • the International Rebate Certificate
  • an auditor’s statement (by a Chinese licensed CPA)

The International Rebate will be calculated and paid in RMB. The Domestic Producer shall be responsible for making payments to the Foreign Producer.

Start date: to be announced
Sunset: N/A
